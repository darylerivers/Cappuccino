### Option 3: Registered Investment Advisor (RIA) - HARD

**What**: Legally manage other people's money (strangers).

**Pros**:
- ‚úÖ Can advertise publicly
- ‚úÖ Unlimited scalability
- ‚úÖ Professional legitimacy
- ‚úÖ Can charge proper fees

**Cons**:
- ‚ùå Heavy regulatory burden (SEC/state registration)
- ‚ùå Expensive ($10K-$50K/year in compliance)
- ‚ùå Need Series 65 license
- ‚ùå Ongoing reporting requirements
- ‚ùå Fiduciary duty (lawsuits if you mess up)

**Requirements**:
- Capital: $50K-$100K (startup costs + 6 months runway)
- Time: 6-12 months (licensing, registration, setup)
- Legal: RIA registration, compliance officer, legal counsel
- Track record: 12-24 months audited live trading
- Licenses: Series 65 (Investment Advisor)

**Income Potential**:
- $1M AUM @ 2/20: $20K + $50K = $70K/year
- $5M AUM @ 2/20: $100K + $250K = $350K/year
- $10M AUM @ 2/20: $200K + $500K = $700K/year

**Verdict**: ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Serious commitment. Only if you want a real fund management business.
